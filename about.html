About

I'm a Master in Development Studies student at the Graduate Institute, Geneva, where I'm equipping myself to support social and economic justice. I hold a B.A. (Adv.) in Global Political Economy from the University of Manitoba, from which I graduated as a President's Scholar for academic excellence (one of 34 out of a class of 3,000). I have also previously studied abroad at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.

My coursework focuses on migration policy and governance; within the field, I'm most interested in labour migration and forced migration. At the Graduate Institute, I sit on the executive of the Migration Initiative, a student group that promotes engagement with, and raises awareness of, issues related to migration. I hope to eventually practice in human development and human rights advocacy, especially for migrants and marginalized populations.

Outside of academia, I'm passionate about education and service. I currently serve as the chairperson of the Youth Parliament of Manitoba, a non-partisan charitable organization that runs a model parliament to engage youth in the democratic process. I'm also a member of Prairie Asian Organizers, a community group that organizes in solidarity with fellow BIPOC communities.

Some of my extracurricular highlights include earning Best Delegate at the 2019 East Asian Model United Nations Conference in Macau, serving as an ambassador for Folklorama's Chinese Pavilion, and representing students on the University of Manitoba's Senate for three years. I'm also a certified lifeguard/swimming instructor and a nostalgic high school a cappella singer. In my spare time, I enjoy hand-lettering, sightseeing, and gaming.
