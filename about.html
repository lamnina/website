About

I was born in Winnipeg, Canada, where I was raised by first-generation Vietnamese immigrants. I'm currently a Master in Development Studies at the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies, where I'm equipping myself to support social and economic justice.

I hold a B.A. (Adv.) in Global Political Economy from the University of Manitoba, from which I graduated as a President's Scholar (one of 34 out of a class of 3,000). During my time there, I completed an exchange at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, and was involved as a student leader in many capacities:

  • I served as a student representative at the university- and faculty-wide level for three years, advocating for student interests vis-à-vis academic policies;
  • I was a student ambassador for the Faculty of Arts for three years, volunteering at all major recruitment events and Orientation;
  • I was a member of the inaugural cohort of the President's Student Leadership Program, and a participant in several volunteer programs through which I acted as a peer mentor and language partner; and
  • I volunteered hundreds of hours at numerous events, including as a team leader at Orientation, a facilitator for poverty awareness workshops, and the volunteer captain at the annual career fair.

At the Graduate Institute, my coursework focuses on migration policy and governance; within the field, I'm most interested in labour migration and forced migration. I also sit on the executive of the Migration Initiative, a student group that serves Geneva's migrant communities. I hope to eventually practice in human development and human rights advocacy, especially for migrants and marginalized populations.

Outside of academia, I'm deeply passionate about education and service. I currently lead the Youth Parliament of Manitoba as their chairperson, where I work to equip youth with the skills and knowledge to be civically engaged. I'm also a member of Prairie Asian Organizers, through which I help facilitate community workshops on understanding the "model minority" myth and dismantling racism.

In my spare time, I love hand-lettering, sightseeing, and singing.
